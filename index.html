About Me


Aspiring Computer Programmer

Hi, my name is Cate Defa. I am currently a senior at Park City High School who plans to major in Computer Science. I am a hardworking student with a passion for learning.

Throughout my four years of high school I have taken various classes to further my education including Programming 1, AP Computer Science Priciples, AP Computer Science A, and PCCAPS App and Technology Solutions. These classes exposed me to languages such as Python, Java, Java Script, and HTML. Additionally, through PCCAPS, I learned effective communication skills and improved both my professionalism and collaborative skills.

Last summer, I also had the opportunity to participate in the Women Tech Council's 2-week "SheTech Explorer" summer internship. I was able to speak directly with women working in Utah's tech industry and learned about all the different STEM careers I can pursue in the future.

Learning Outcomes


Collaboration

Being on various sports team as well as group projects has taught me a lot about collaboration. When it comes to soccer, you must effectively work with ten other players in order to win. Similarly, my PCCAPS project would not have been possible had it not been for the two other peers that I worked with to complete it.

The best way to eat an elephant is one bite at a time, rather than trying to tackle it all at once. Through my various team/group experiences, I have learned the value in working with others and splitting up tasks amongst group members. Collaboration increases efficiency and creativity. There is no "i" in team.


Professionalism & Communication

PCCAPS involved weekly meetings with a client. I had to make sure I was always prepared beforehand including having a detailed meeting presentation to present to them, dressing in a professional manner, and acting and speaking in a mature manner. I also had to send various emails to them over the course of the class. I learned valuable skills when it comes to formatting a professional and formal email like using lots of bullet points and underlines.


Website for Pick Up PC

My PCCAPS project was to develop a website for Recycle Utah and their "Pick Up PC" Program. To create the project I had to learn how to use GitHub, VS Code, HTML, and CSS. The knowledge and skills I acquired from this class were immeasurable. I learned how to develop a website, new coding languages, and to be self reliant when it comes to finding solutions to my problems. I am excited to see how I can apply my newly acquired skills to all of my future endeavors and projects!
